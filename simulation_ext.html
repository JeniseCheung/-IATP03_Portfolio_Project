Overview


Gloomy Day Clean-Up is a simulation game that involves organizing a messy room based on instructions displayed on the screen. The goal is for players to complete the tasks assigned to them, resulting in a neat and organized room.

The objective is to transform our ideas into a digital form using Java programming with concepts such as factory and decorator patterns, custom shape creation, sound effects, exception handling, GUI widgets, and code organization using packages. This project provides an opportunity to showcase creativity while meeting technical requirements.

Process


Planning and Proposal

Initially, I planned a proposal of the concept explaining the project idea, user interaction, main process and a storyboard. As someone who related to cleaning as a mental refresher that can improve my mood, I hoped to encourage others to clean their rooms.

Concept Proposal

Drawings and Animation

To create the simulation, I used Sketchbook to draw each component while referring to internet images for color schemes. I used colours that resulted in a gloomy yet calming atmosphere. I designed various animations, including spiders moving and changing weather featuring moving rain and clouds.

Drawings created in Sketchbook

Development

Regarding the code, I used packages to organize it and created various states. I have a total of 19 states using the FSM model for the interactions, sounds and various animations. I also ran my code regularly throughout the coding process to check and ensure it was working properly. I utilized perlin noise to create the dust object to showcase my proficiency. Additionally, I used the factory pattern to design both clickable and unclickable objects and arraylists, and the decorator pattern to construct painting objects for GUI widget utilization.

This is a sample code. It demonstrates the use of the factory pattern to initialize objects in Java

Audio

I took into account the correlation between animations and sounds during the development. The majority of the audio was recorded through my phone's voice memos app, while I also obtained additional sounds from external sources such as Fesilyan Studios. Each interaction was accompanied by a sound effect and I incorporated background ambient music to establish the atmosphere and tone. The sounds were designed to assist users in associating particular sounds with on-screen actions. I added the audio last once I confirmed that the code was working correctly.

Audio from Voice Memos Application

Challenges


Issues

While working on the project, I struggled the most with recursion (creating fractal objects). I also encountered a challenge of ensuring that all animations were working correctly with each interaction. I faced issues with transitioning states as some of them were not functioning properly, and also encountered problems when implementing arraylists with the factory pattern. Due to the sheer amount of code involved, identifying minor mistakes was a bit difficult.

Resolution

To overcome this issue, I referred to my lecture notes and labs to learn and familiarize myself with creating fractal objects with recursion. Additionally, I carefully reviewed my code and identified small mistakes such as case-sensitivity in strings. For the factory pattern and arraylists, I experimented with different versions of the code until I found a solution and noted down the reasons for my past unsuccessful attempts.

Reflection


What I've Learned

By participating in this project, I gained knowledge about basic knowledge of Java programming language including concepts such as inheritance, pull-up operations and polymorphism. I’ve also gained insight into the game design process and factors to consider when creating games for a specific audience. For this particular project, my target audience was individuals who enjoy serene “cozy/ wholesome games”.

Areas of Improvement

Upon reflecting on the project, I noticed that there is room for improvement in terms of the smoothness of the simulation. I am unsure whether the lag was due to my laptop or not, as it was running smoothly in the beginning, but became choppy towards the end, especially during screen recording. Moreover, I wish to include more animations and interactions to make the gameplay longer and more enjoyable.

Final Thoughts

I aim to use the skills and I hope to work on more similar projects and create games that I am passionate about. Overall, the project is a success.
